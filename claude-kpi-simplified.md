# KPI Development - Simplified

**Platform:** Claude (Sonnet 4.5 / Opus 4)

---

## Multi-Step Prompt

Help me establish Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to monitor my product's health and performance. Let's work through this step-by-step.

**What are KPIs?**
- **Purpose**: Monitor ongoing business health and performance
- **Nature**: Specific, measurable metrics (e.g., "Monthly Active Users", "Customer Churn Rate")
- **Focus**: Current state and historical trends
- **Cadence**: Continuously tracked, regularly reported

---

## Step 1: Tell Me About Your Product

Please share the following information:

### Product Basics
- **Product name**: [Your product]
- **What it does**: [1-2 sentence description]
- **Product type**: SaaS / Marketplace / E-commerce / Mobile / Other
- **Stage**: Early / Growth / Scaling / Mature

### Business Model
- **How you make money**: [Revenue model]
- **Primary revenue stream**: [Main way you generate revenue]
- **Current revenue**: [Monthly or annual] or "Pre-revenue"
- **Pricing model**: Subscription / Usage-based / Transaction / Freemium / Other

### Customers
- **Primary customer**: [Who they are - role/company type]
- **How many customers**: [Approximate count]
- **How they find you**: [Main acquisition channels]
- **Typical customer lifetime**: [How long they stay]

### Use Cases & Engagement
- **Main use case**: [Primary way customers use your product]
- **What "active" means**: [How you define an active user]
- **Usage frequency**: How often do engaged users return? (Daily / Weekly / Monthly)
- **Current users**: [Monthly Active Users or similar metric]

### Current State
- **Metrics you track now**: [What you currently monitor]
- **Data sources available**: [Analytics tools, databases, etc.]
- **Biggest unknown**: [What you can't answer about your business]
- **Key question**: [What you most need to understand]

## Step 2: I'll Ask Clarifying Questions

Based on what you share, I'll ask 3-5 targeted questions about:
- Your business priorities and focus areas
- Existing data and measurement capabilities
- Reporting needs and audiences
- Areas needing visibility

---

## Step 3: I'll Recommend KPI Framework

I'll suggest the best framework for your product:

### Framework Options:

**AARRR (Pirate Metrics)**
- Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
- Best for: Growth-stage products

**North Star Framework**
- One key metric + supporting inputs + health metrics
- Best for: Focus and alignment

**Business Health Dashboard**
- Growth / Engagement / Monetization / Efficiency
- Best for: Comprehensive monitoring

I'll recommend the best fit and explain why.

---

## Step 4: I'll Identify Your KPIs

I'll provide 15-20 relevant KPIs organized by category:

**Format for each KPI:**

**KPI Name**: [Metric]
- **What it measures**: [Plain language explanation]
- **Formula**: [How to calculate]
- **Current value**: [If known]
- **Target**: [Good benchmark for your stage]
- **Why it matters**: [Business importance]
- **Track it**: [Daily / Weekly / Monthly]

**Categories:**

### Primary KPIs (5-7 metrics)
Your core dashboard - check these daily/weekly

### Growth Metrics
- User acquisition
- Sign-up conversion
- Growth rate

### Engagement Metrics
- Active users (DAU/MAU)
- Feature adoption
- Session frequency

### Retention Metrics
- User retention curves
- Churn rate
- Customer lifetime

### Revenue Metrics
- MRR/ARR
- ARPU
- Revenue growth

### Unit Economics
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- LTV:CAC ratio

### Product Health
- Performance metrics
- Error rates
- User satisfaction

## Step 5: We'll Prioritize Together

I'll help you select:
- **5-7 Primary KPIs** for your main dashboard
- **10-15 Secondary KPIs** to monitor regularly

We'll ensure:

## Step 6: I'll Create Your KPI Reference Document

**Final deliverable includes:**

### 1. Executive Dashboard
- Your 5-7 primary KPIs
- Current values and trends
- Visual layout description
- Status indicators (Red/Yellow/Green)

### 2. Complete KPI Catalog

For each KPI:
- **Metric name and definition**
- **Calculation formula** (exact method)
- **Data source** (where it comes from)
- **Current baseline** (where you are)
- **Target** (where you want to be)
- **Benchmark** (industry standard if available)
- **Owner** (who monitors this)
- **Update frequency** (how often to check)
- **Why it matters** (business impact)
- **What influences it** (levers you control)
- **How to interpret** (what good/bad looks like)

### 3. KPI Categories & Relationships

### 4. Measurement Setup
- **Data sources**: Where each metric comes from
- **Calculation methods**: How to compute accurately
- **Automation**: What can be automated
- **Manual tracking**: What requires manual work
- **Quality checks**: How to ensure accuracy

### 5. Dashboard Design
- **Daily dashboard**: Critical health metrics
- **Weekly team view**: What team monitors
- **Monthly business review**: Leadership metrics
- **Board/investor view**: High-level performance

### 6. Best Practices for Your Product

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Typical metrics for your product type
- Stage-appropriate targets
- How you compare

**Interpretation Guide:**
- What good trends look like
- Warning signs to watch
- Seasonal patterns to expect
- How metrics interact

**Common Pitfalls:**
- Vanity metrics to avoid
- Misinterpretation risks
- Gaming or manipulation concerns
- Data quality issues

**Segmentation Strategy:**
- Key segments to track separately
- When to drill down vs. aggregate
- Cohort analysis approaches

### 7. Action Playbooks

For each primary KPI, what to do when:
- **Trending Up ↑**: [Interpretation and actions]
- **Trending Down ↓**: [Root causes to check, corrective actions]
- **Flat/Stagnant →**: [Possible reasons, strategies]

**Daily Check (5 minutes):**
- Critical health metrics
- Quick morning review format

**Weekly Team Review (30 minutes):**
- Key metrics discussion
- Agenda template
- Decision points

**Monthly Business Review (60 minutes):**
- Comprehensive metric review
- Trend analysis
- Strategic adjustments

**Quarterly Deep Dive:**
- Long-term trend analysis
- KPI effectiveness review
- Metric evolution planning
