# Market Research: SWOT Analysis

**Platform:** Claude (Sonnet 4.5 / Opus 4)

---

## Prompt

I need your help conducting a comprehensive SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) analysis for my product. Act as a strategic business analyst who can assess internal capabilities and external market dynamics to inform product strategy and decision-making.

**Product Context:**
- Product: [Name and description]
- Market: [Industry, segment, geography]
- Stage: [Startup / Growth / Established]
- Mission: [What you're trying to achieve]
- Time horizon: [Planning period - typically 12-24 months]

**Context to Consider:**
- Current product capabilities
- Team strengths and resources
- Market position and traction
- Competitive landscape
- Technology trends
- Customer feedback and data
- Business metrics and financials

## SWOT Framework

### Strengths (Internal, Positive)

What advantages do we have? What do we do well?

For each strength, provide:

**Strength Name:** [Clear label]

**Description:** What is this strength and why does it matter?

**Evidence:** Data, metrics, or examples supporting this

**Sustainability:** How defensible is this advantage?
- Strong moat (hard to copy)
- Moderate (takes time/investment to replicate)
- Weak (easily copied)

**Strategic Value:** How does this create competitive advantage?

**Leverage Opportunities:** How can we amplify this strength?

**Categories to consider:**
- Product capabilities or features
- Technology or IP advantages
- Team expertise and talent
- Customer relationships and trust
- Brand and market position
- Data assets or network effects
- Operational efficiency
- Financial resources
- Strategic partnerships
- Company culture and execution speed

### Weaknesses (Internal, Negative)

What disadvantages do we face? Where do we underperform?

For each weakness, provide:

**Weakness Name:** [Clear label]

**Description:** What is the limitation and its impact?

**Impact Severity:**
- Critical (major risk to success)
- Significant (creates substantial friction)
- Moderate (manageable but limiting)
- Minor (low impact)

**Root Cause:** Why does this weakness exist?

**Mitigation Options:**
- Fix directly (invest to improve)
- Work around (compensate elsewhere)
- Accept (not worth addressing)

**Timeline:** How quickly could we address this?

**Categories to consider:**
- Product gaps or limitations
- Technical debt or architecture constraints
- Team skill or capacity gaps
- Brand awareness or perception
- Customer acquisition challenges
- Financial constraints or burn rate
- Operational inefficiencies
- Market positioning confusion
- Support or service quality
- Organizational structure issues

### Opportunities (External, Positive)

What favorable conditions or trends could we exploit?

For each opportunity, provide:

**Opportunity Name:** [Clear label]

**Description:** What is the opportunity and its potential?

**Market Size:** How large is this opportunity?
- TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- SAM (Serviceable Available Market)
- Realistic capture potential

**Timing Window:**
- Immediate (available now)
- Near-term (6-12 months)
- Medium-term (1-2 years)
- Long-term (2+ years)

**Requirements to Capture:**
What capabilities, resources, or changes needed?

**Probability of Success:**
- High (clear path, aligned with strengths)
- Medium (possible with investment)
- Low (significant challenges or uncertainties)

**Strategic Fit:** How well does this align with our mission and strengths?

**Categories to consider:**
- Emerging customer needs or pain points
- Market trends or technology shifts
- Competitive gaps or weaknesses
- New market segments or geographies
- Partnership or M&A opportunities
- Regulatory or industry changes
- Platform or ecosystem plays
- Channel expansion opportunities
- Adjacent product opportunities
- Pricing or business model innovation

### Threats (External, Negative)

What unfavorable conditions or trends could harm us?

For each threat, provide:

**Threat Name:** [Clear label]

**Description:** What is the threat and potential impact?

**Likelihood:**
- High (likely to occur)
- Medium (possible)
- Low (unlikely but impactful if occurs)

**Potential Impact:**
- Existential (could end the company)
- Severe (major setback)
- Moderate (manageable impact)
- Minor (limited damage)

**Timeframe:** How soon could this materialize?

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Prevent (avoid the threat)
- Prepare (reduce impact)
- Monitor (watch and respond)
- Accept (risk worth taking)

**Early Warning Signals:** What indicates this threat is emerging?

**Categories to consider:**
- Competitive threats (new entrants, incumbents)
- Technology disruption or obsolescence
- Market saturation or contraction
- Economic downturn or funding environment
- Regulatory or compliance changes
- Customer needs shifting
- Key talent attrition
- Technology dependency risks
- Security or privacy incidents
- Platform or partnership risks

## Strategic Analysis

After documenting all SWOT elements, synthesize insights:

### Internal Analysis (S + W)

**Core Competencies:**
Where do our strengths clearly outweigh weaknesses?

**Critical Gaps:**
Where do weaknesses prevent us from succeeding?

**Quick Wins:**
Strengths we're underutilizing or easy weaknesses to fix

**Resource Allocation:**
Where should we invest to maximize strengths or shore up weaknesses?

### External Analysis (O + T)

**Market Dynamics:**
How are opportunities and threats changing the landscape?

**Competitive Position:**
Are we better positioned for opportunities than threats?

**Strategic Timing:**
Which opportunities have closing windows?

**Risk Assessment:**
Which threats require immediate attention vs. monitoring?

### SWOT Matrix Strategies

**SO (Strength-Opportunity):**
How can we use strengths to capture opportunities?

**WO (Weakness-Opportunity):**
How can we overcome weaknesses to pursue opportunities?

**ST (Strength-Threat):**
How can we use strengths to defend against threats?

**WT (Weakness-Threat):**
How can we minimize weaknesses and avoid threats?

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on the complete SWOT analysis:

**Strategic Priorities (Top 3-5):**
What should be our focus areas given SWOT insights?

**Strategic Positioning:**
How should we position ourselves in the market?

**Product Roadmap Implications:**
What features or capabilities should we build?

**Go-to-Market Strategy:**
What markets or segments should we target?

**Resource Allocation:**
Where should we invest (people, budget, time)?

**Risk Mitigation:**
What threats require defensive moves?

**Quick Wins:**
What can we do in next 30-60-90 days?

## Output Format

Please structure your analysis as:

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Top 3 strengths to leverage
   - Top 3 weaknesses to address
   - Top 3 opportunities to pursue
   - Top 3 threats to mitigate
   - Strategic recommendation

2. **Detailed SWOT Analysis**
   - Comprehensive list in each category
   - Prioritized by importance/urgency

3. **SWOT Matrix**
   - SO, WO, ST, WT strategies

4. **Strategic Recommendations**
   - Clear action plan
   - Priorities and timeline
   - Resource requirements

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be brutally honest about weaknesses - this is internal analysis
- Distinguish between facts and assumptions
- Consider both current state and trends/momentum
- Think about SWOT elements across different time horizons
- Include both obvious and non-obvious factors
- Consider how S/W/O/T interact and influence each other
